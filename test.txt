[<p><b>Yeshe Dorje</b> (1676–1702) was the eleventh <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyalwa_Karmapa" title="Gyalwa Karmapa">Gyalwa Karmapa</a>, head of the <a href="/wiki/Kagyu" title="Kagyu">Kagyu</a> School of <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Buddhism" title="Tibetan Buddhism">Tibetan Buddhism</a>.
</p>, 

<p>Yeshe Dorje was born in Mayshö, <a href="/wiki/Kham" title="Kham">Kham</a>. He was discovered by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Minjur_Dorje&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Minjur Dorje (page does not exist)">Minjur Dorje</a> and recognized by Shamar Yeshe Nyinpo, the seventh <a href="/wiki/Shamarpa" title="Shamarpa">Shamarpa</a>. Yeshe Dorje was transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Tibet" title="Central Tibet">Central Tibet</a> for his education and was ordained in the <a href="/wiki/Tsurphu_Monastery" title="Tsurphu Monastery">Tsurphu Monastery</a>. He received an education both in the Kagyu school as well as the <a href="/wiki/Nyingma" title="Nyingma">Nyingma</a> school. Yeshe Dorje integrated the teaching of Tercho by <a href="/wiki/Padmasambhava" title="Padmasambhava">Padmasambhava</a> in the Kagyu school.
</p>, 

<p>He was the shortest-lived of all the Karmapas. Like the 10th Karmapa before him, he left a detailed letter referring to his next incarnation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>]
